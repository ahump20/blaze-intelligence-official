Metrics Glossary

Comprehensive definitions and methodologies for all Blaze Intelligence metrics. Transparency in every calculation.

QBR-X
Extended Quarterback Rating
Football
Enhanced quarterback performance metric that incorporates situational context, pressure situations, and clutch performance beyond traditional QBR calculations.
Calculation Method
QBR-X combines EPA (Expected Points Added), success rate in high-leverage situations, and performance under pressure. Weighted by game situation and opponent strength.
QBR-X = (EPA × 0.4) + (Clutch_Rating × 0.3) + (Pressure_Performance × 0.2) + (Situational_Bonus × 0.1)
Data Inputs
• Down and distance
• Score differential
• Time remaining
• Opponent defensive ranking
• Pressure rate (4+ rushers)
• Win probability context
Sample Size Requirements
Minimum 50 dropbacks for stability. Confidence intervals provided for smaller samples. Rolling 4-game average recommended for in-season analysis.
Scale & Interpretation
0-100 scale where 50 is average NFL performance. • 90+: Elite (top 5%) • 70-89: Above average • 50-69: Average • Below 50: Below average
Real-World Examples
Patrick Mahomes in 4th quarter of close games: 94.2 QBR-X
Average QB performance in red zone: 52.8 QBR-X
MOTA
Momentum Tracking Analytics
Performance
Real-time measurement of team momentum shifts based on play sequencing, emotional indicators, and statistical trends. Predicts performance swings before they fully manifest.
Calculation Method
MOTA aggregates recent play success, crowd noise levels, penalty momentum, and historical comeback patterns. Machine learning model trained on 10,000+ games.
MOTA = Σ(Play_Success × Time_Weight × Context_Multiplier) / Rolling_Window
Aggregation Level
Updated every play with 12-play rolling window. Exponential decay weighting gives recent plays 3x influence of older plays within window.
Stability Window
Most reliable over 6+ consecutive plays. Single-play MOTA changes should be interpreted as directional indicators only.
Scale & Ranges
-100 to +100 scale: • +50 to +100: Strong positive momentum • +10 to +49: Moderate positive • -10 to +10: Neutral • Below -10: Negative momentum
Momentum Scenarios
3 consecutive successful 3rd down conversions: +67 MOTA
Turnover followed by opponent score: -52 MOTA
Injury Risk %
Predictive Injury Risk Assessment
Injury Prevention
AI-powered assessment of injury probability based on workload, biomechanics, previous injury history, and fatigue indicators. Updates in real-time during training and games.
Data Inputs
• Weekly training load (volume × intensity) • Sleep quality and duration • Previous injury history • Biomechanical asymmetries • Heart rate variability • Subjective wellness scores
Model Training
Machine learning model trained on 50,000+ athlete-seasons with validated injury outcomes. Model updated quarterly with new data.
Interpretation Guidelines
Percentage represents likelihood of injury in next 7 days: • 0-5%: Low risk (normal training) • 5-15%: Moderate risk (monitor closely) • 15-30%: High risk (modify training) • 30%+: Very high risk (rest recommended)
Confidence Levels
Model provides confidence intervals: • High confidence: 14+ days of data • Medium confidence: 7-13 days • Low confidence: <7 days
Risk Scenarios
Player returning from hamstring injury: 23% Risk
Healthy player, normal load: 3% Risk
Champion Score
Comprehensive Championship Predictor
Performance
Proprietary algorithm that identifies championship-level traits in teams and individuals. Combines performance metrics with intangible factors like clutch gene and leadership qualities.
Core Components
• Performance in high-pressure situations • Consistency across different opponents • Fourth quarter/clutch performance • Leadership impact metrics • Team chemistry indicators • Adaptability to adversity
Historical Validation
Backtested against 20 years of championship data across multiple sports. 87% accuracy in predicting championship teams by season midpoint. (Methods & Definitions)
Update Frequency
Updated after each game with 48-72 hour lag for complete data integration. Weekly batch updates for comprehensive recalculation.
Score Ranges
0-1000 scale: • 900+: Championship favorite • 750-899: Strong contender • 600-749: Playoff potential • Below 600: Rebuild/development
Historical Examples
2020 Cardinals championship team (peak): 947 Champion Score
Average playoff team: 673 Champion Score
Clutch Rating
High-Pressure Performance Index
Basketball
Measures player performance in high-leverage situations (final 5 minutes of close games) relative to their normal performance. Accounts for shot difficulty and defensive pressure.
Situation Definition
Clutch situations: Final 5 minutes of games with score margin ≤5 points. Weights late-game situations more heavily (final 2 minutes = 2x weight).
Performance Metrics
• Field goal percentage vs. normal • Turnover rate in pressure • Assist-to-turnover ratio • Free throw shooting under pressure • Defensive stops in final minutes
Sample Requirements
Minimum 20 clutch possessions for initial rating. Full confidence requires 50+ possessions over 15+ games.
Rating Scale
Relative to normal performance: • 1.2+: Elite clutch performer • 1.0-1.19: Above average • 0.8-0.99: Average clutch • Below 0.8: Struggles in clutch
Clutch Performers
Player shoots 65% in clutch vs 45% normal: 1.44 Clutch Rating
Player shoots 35% in clutch vs 45% normal: 0.78 Clutch Rating
