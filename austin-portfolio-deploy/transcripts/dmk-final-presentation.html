[Austin]: Good afternoon. I want to start with a simple premise: You don't need more dashboards. You need plays - specific, executable decisions that change outcomes.

[Austin]: Let me show you what I mean with actual funnel mathematics. Here's our trial-to-paid conversion over the last 90 days. Notice I'm not showing you averages - I'm showing you the distribution.

[Executive]: Why does the distribution matter more than the average?

[Austin]: Because averages hide the signal. Look at this bimodal distribution - we have two distinct customer segments converting at different rates. The average tells us 23%. The distribution tells us we have a 45% converter segment and a 8% converter segment. That's actionable.

[Austin]: Now, let's talk about kill criteria. Before we run any experiment, we pre-commit to thresholds. If conversion drops below 18%, we kill. If CAC exceeds $127, we kill. No negotiation, no "one more week." The decision is already made.

[Executive]: How do you prevent teams from gaming these thresholds?

[Austin]: Simple - the thresholds are set by the model, not the team. We use Bayesian updating with a skeptical prior. The model literally doesn't believe good news without overwhelming evidence.

[Austin]: Here's our proof hierarchy. Level 1: Anecdotal evidence - customer says they love it. Level 2: Behavioral evidence - usage data shows engagement. Level 3: Economic evidence - they pay and retain. Level 4: Causal evidence - controlled experiment shows our intervention caused the outcome.

[Austin]: We only make strategic bets on Level 3 or 4 evidence. Everything else is an experiment with kill criteria.

[Executive]: What's your confidence interval on these projections?

[Austin]: 90% confidence we'll hit between $2.3M and $2.8M in Q3, assuming no black swan events. But more importantly, we have response plans for scenarios outside that range. If we're tracking below $2.3M by July 15th, we execute Plan B - which is already documented and resourced.

[Austin]: The ask is simple: $250K for the next quarter, two senior engineers, and weekly kill-criteria reviews. If we miss any threshold, you pull the plug, no questions asked. But if we hit them, we've de-risked a $10M opportunity.
