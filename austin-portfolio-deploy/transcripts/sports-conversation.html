[Austin]: So when we're talking about player development, the first thing I always establish is trust. You can't teach technique to someone who doesn't believe you understand their struggle.

[Player]: That makes sense. I've had coaches who just throw drills at me without understanding what I'm actually trying to fix.

[Austin]: Exactly. Let me show you what I mean. When you're at the plate, what's the one thing you're thinking about right before the pitch?

[Player]: The difference is immediate. I'm picking up the ball maybe 5 feet earlier with "hunt."

[Austin]: That's pattern recognition at work. Your brain already knows how to track objects - we're just giving it better software. Now let's layer in the next piece...

[Player]: This is completely different from how I've been taught before. It's like you're programming my instincts instead of overriding them.

[Austin]: Exactly. Championship performance isn't about perfect mechanics - it's about perfect instincts under pressure. The mechanics follow when the mind is clear.
