[Austin]: Thank you for joining today. I want to present a NIL partnership framework that creates value for all stakeholders - the athletes, the university, DCTF, and your brand - while maintaining full compliance with state law and NCAA guidelines.

[Austin]: Let's start with context. The NIL landscape in Texas is mature but fragmented. Athletes have opportunities, but lack strategic guidance. Brands want authentic partnerships, but fear compliance risk. We're proposing a structure that solves both problems.

[Partner]: What makes this different from other NIL collectives or agencies?

[Austin]: Three things. First, we're not a collective - we're a strategic partnership facilitator. Second, we focus on skill development alongside brand building. Third, every activation includes performance metrics tied to actual athletic improvement, not just social media reach.

[Austin]: Here's the asset mix. Level 1: Social media presence - 4 posts per month, authentic to the athlete's voice. Level 2: Content creation - behind-the-scenes training videos showing your product in use. Level 3: Community engagement - youth camps and clinics wearing your brand.

[Partner]: How do we ensure authenticity? We don't want it to feel forced.

[Austin]: Each athlete gets a content coach - not to script them, but to help them find their authentic voice. We use a "Story Bank" system where athletes share real moments, then we help them craft those into content. No templates, no corporate speak.

[Austin]: Measurement is critical. We track four categories: Brand metrics - awareness, sentiment, consideration. Engagement metrics - not just likes, but meaningful interactions. Performance metrics - the athlete's on-field improvement. Business metrics - direct attribution to sales where possible.

[Austin]: For compliance, we have three checkpoints. Pre-activation: Legal review of all contracts. During activation: Weekly compliance audits. Post-activation: Full documentation for university and state reporting.

[Partner]: What's the risk if an athlete gets injured or transfers?

[Austin]: Built into the contract. Injury: Content pivots to recovery journey - often more engaging than game highlights. Transfer: Clean exit clause with content library rights preserved. We've modeled 20% athlete turnover into the pricing.

[Austin]: Investment structure: $180K per year covers 6 athletes across 3 sports. That's $30K per athlete - competitive but not market-distorting. 60% goes directly to athletes, 40% covers production, compliance, and management.

[Austin]: Success criteria are clear: 15% brand awareness lift in the 18-34 Texas demographic. 3.5% engagement rate on athlete content. 2x ROI within 18 months through direct attribution and brand value creation.

[Partner]: How do we scale this if it works?

[Austin]: The framework is designed to scale. Year 2: Expand to 12 athletes. Year 3: Multi-university partnerships. The compliance infrastructure and content systems are built to handle 50+ athletes without proportional cost increases.
